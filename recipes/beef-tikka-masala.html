Beef Tikka Masala

Description

This is a recipe from the old age English-Indian cuisine which for decades has been the staple food for many of night owls and Saturday night takeaway goers. Mostly eaten by those who are inebriated but can also be enjoyed by the sober a like. If its not all down your pretty shirt in addition to all over your mouth by the time you are half way through the meal, then you are not eating it right.

Ingredients

  • 907 grams Certified Angus Beef ® top round or bottom round roast 1/2-inch dice
  • 30 grams all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 2 tsp coarse kosher salt
  • 1 tsp freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1 tsp ground tumeric
  • ½ tsp ground coriander
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper
  • 2—3 tbsp vegetable or canola oil
  • 1 diced onion
  • 2 cans diced tomatoes
  • 152 grams plain Greek yogurt
  • Suggested accompaniments: basmati rice, naan bread

Instructions

  1. With paper towels, pat bottom round dices dry. Combine flour, curry, salt, pepper, turmeric and coriander; set aside.
  2. Heat oil in heavy-bottom pan. Sear diced beef over medium-high heat in batches until browned; transfer to slow cooker.
  3. Add onions and reserved spiced flour to pan and stir. Add tomatoes and mix; transfer to slow cooker. Cook 5-6 hours on low.
  4. Stir in yogurt prior to serving. Serve with prepared rice and naan if desired.
